## What Is Context Compacting?

Claude has a **context window** — a limited amount of text it can hold in memory during one conversation. When a conversation gets too long, Anthropic automatically **compacts** (summarizes) the earlier parts of the conversation to free up space. This is done automatically in the background without asking.

The compacted summary replaces the full conversation history with a shorter version. Claude continues working from that summary — but details that were not included in the summary are **permanently lost** for that session.

---

## The Problem — What Gets Lost

|Lost|Why It Matters for HAP|
|---|---|
|Exact affiliate links (amzn.to/xxxxx)|Claude uses wrong or placeholder links in new articles|
|Published article URLs|Internal links in new articles point to wrong paths|
|Product image URLs|Claude cannot insert correct images into HTML|
|Decisions made mid-session|e.g. "we decided to noindex outdoor sub-categories" — gone|
|Rank Math scores already fixed|Claude thinks issue is still open and re-flags it|
|Canonical tags already set|Claude re-flags duplicate content issue|
|Article status (live vs scheduled)|Claude treats scheduled articles as unwritten|
|Template assignments|Claude uses wrong template for new article|

---

## The Benefits — Why Compacting Exists

|Benefit|Detail|
|---|---|
|Longer sessions|Without compacting, sessions would hit the limit and stop completely|
|Continued context|Claude keeps the general project awareness even after compacting|
|No manual action needed|Happens automatically — you do not need to do anything|
|Preserves high-level memory|Site name, niche, brand colors, goals are usually retained|
|Allows multi-hour work sessions|You can keep working in one conversation for hours|

## The HAP Compacting Protocol — What To Do

### Before Starting a New Session (2 min)

Always paste this at the start of every new chat:

```
Fetch my HAP vault: https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Kikova4u/OBHAPVault/refs/heads/master/Index.md
```

This gives Claude the full site status, pending actions, and article URLs immediately — no relying on memory.

---

### When You Notice Compacting Happened

Signs Claude was compacted:

- Claude asks for affiliate links it already had
- Claude re-flags issues that were already fixed
- Claude forgets which articles are live vs scheduled
- Claude uses placeholder `amzn.to/xxxxx` links
- Claude loses track of the current task list

**Fix:** Paste the vault URL again + paste the relevant product data from your Excel tracker.

---

### What To Keep in the Vault (Always Up To Date)

The vault is your protection against compacting. Every session, update these:

|File|What to update|
|---|---|
|`Index.md`|Mark articles as live, add new URLs, tick off pending actions|
|`AFFILIATE-TRACKER.md`|Add new affiliate links after each article|
|`CONTENT-TRACKER.md`|Move articles from Planned → Published|
|`INTERNAL-LINKING.md`|Add new article URLs for internal linking|

**Rule:** If something was decided in a session — write it to the vault before closing the chat. Anything not in the vault will be lost on the next compact.

When starting a work session for a specific article, always give Claude:

This ensures Claude has everything it needs even if it was just compacted.

## What Anthropic Says About Compacting

- Compacting is **automatic** and happens when the conversation exceeds roughly 80-90% of the context window
- The summary is generated by Claude itself — it tries to keep the most important information
- **Strategic decisions, specific numbers, and URLs are the most commonly lost** because they look like detail rather than structure
- There is no way to turn off compacting — it is a core part of how Claude manages long conversations
- The **project memory system** (userMemories) partially compensates — key facts saved to memory persist across sessions even after compacting

---

## HAP-Specific Rules to Prevent Compacting Damage

1. **Never rely on Claude remembering affiliate links** — always have your Excel tracker open
2. **Paste the vault URL at the start of every session** — non-negotiable
3. **After fixing an issue in WordPress** — tell Claude "done, mark as fixed" so it updates its task list
4. **After publishing an article** — give Claude the live URL immediately so it can use it for internal links
5. **Keep sessions focused** — one article per session when possible — reduces compacting risk
6. **End each session with a summary request** — ask Claude "what should I update in the vault before closing?" to catch anything missed
